⚠ SECTOR: LONG-DURATION BONDS — Rate Risk Traps
The most dangerous trade in any scenario except a confirmed Fed pivot. Long bonds are leveraged bets that Warsh cuts rates soon. If QE is delayed OR the deal fails and inflation re-accelerates, these get destroyed. The 30-year bond lost 50%+ from 2020 to 2023. That can happen again. Wait for the first confirmed cut before touching duration. Watch Groman Signal #3 (10Y yield).
⚠ SECTOR: UNPROFITABLE TECH / HIGH-BURN EV — QE-Dependent
These companies have negative ROIC, burn through cash, and are entirely dependent on cheap capital to fund operations. In your own screener, PLUG showed -79.62% ROIC — the screener itself screamed avoid. Without QE, the funding window closes. High-burn EVs need rates below 3% to refinance. Avoid until Groman signals 1, 3, and 5 all flip green simultaneously.
⚠ SECTOR: CONSUMER CYCLICAL — Discretionary Spending Collapses
When oil stays high, gas eats $200-$400/month of household budgets. Discretionary spending dies first — furniture, cars, gym equipment, restaurants all see demand destruction. These names face a double compression: earnings fall AND investors reprice the multiple down simultaneously. In stagflation, consumer cyclicals can lose 35-65% peak-to-trough.
⚠ SECTOR: REAL ESTATE / REITs — Leveraged Bond Proxies
REITs are essentially bond proxies with leverage. Every 25bps increase in the 10Y yield compresses REIT valuations ~3-5%. Office REITs face a structural catastrophe on top of the rate problem: WFH permanently reduced demand. Medical REITs have tenant stress (Steward Health bankruptcy). Only flip long on REITs AFTER the first confirmed Warsh rate cut AND Groman signal #3 turns green.
⚠ SECTOR: STORY STOCKS / NO MOAT — QE-Era Zombies
The zero-interest-rate policy era funded thousands of companies that had stories but no economics. No pricing power, no ROIC, no Piotroski score worth noting. These are structural shorts in tight-money environments. The QE era that created them is not coming back until Groman signals all flip green. Until then, these are cash incinerators with good PowerPoint decks.
⚠ SECTOR: SMALL-CAP INDUSTRIALS WITH NEGATIVE ROIC TREND — Input Cost Squeeze
Small industrials with weak balance sheets get crushed when input costs (energy, steel, labor) spike and they lack the pricing power to pass those costs through. Your screener's ROIC Trend field identifies the ones deteriorating. Combined with high debt and no moat, these face margin compression from both sides simultaneously in stagflation or bear scenarios.
